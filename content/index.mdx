## About

This digital exhibit transforms how we explore and understand our university's rich heritage. At its heart lies a dynamic, interactive map that allows visitors to witness the remarkable transformation of our campus across fifteen decades. Watch as the original four buildings of the Agricultural and Mechanical College grow into today's sprawling 5,200-acre campus, home to over 70,000 Aggies.

### Features

* **Interactive Campus Map**: Navigate through time with our dynamic mapping system that visualizes the physical growth and architectural evolution of campus. Explore how academic buildings, residence halls, recreational facilities, and iconic landmarks have shaped the Aggie experience across generations.

* **Comprehensive Search**: Our robust search functionality enables you to discover specific buildings, historical periods, architectural styles, or significant events with ease. Whether you're researching the construction of the Academic Building or exploring the development of student life facilities, find exactly what you're looking for.

* **Historical Timelines**: Experience the university's growth through carefully curated timelines that contextualize campus development within broader historical moments—from Reconstruction-era Texas through two World Wars, the Great Depression, the Civil Rights era, and into the digital age.

* **Building Histories** : Each structure tells a story. Discover the architects, construction dates, renovations, and significant events associated with every building on campus. Learn about the people and purposes that have shaped these spaces where Aggies have lived, learned, and grown.


### Acknowledgments

This exhibit draws upon the extensive archival collections housed in Cushing Memorial Library & Archives, including historical photographs, architectural drawings, university records, and personal papers. We extend our gratitude to the countless individuals who have contributed to preserving Texas A&M's history and to the donors whose support makes projects like this possible.
